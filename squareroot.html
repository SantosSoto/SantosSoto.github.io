Lesson 1: Square roots

Chapter 1: square roots

in this chapter we will learn how square roots work root(10). square roots are numbers that when multiplied by itself give you the original number.

Let's talk about an example: root(4). this means that the number that when multiplied by itself gives us 4 is 2 2 * 2 = 4 another example is root(9). this means that the number that when multiplied by itself gives us 9 is 3 3 * 3 = 9

Notes to remember:

- The square root of a number is the opposite of squaring that number.
- The square root of a perfect square is an integer.
- Not all numbers have exact square roots; some are irrational numbers.
- The square root of a negative number is not a real number.
root(-x) = imaginary number
