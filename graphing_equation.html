    Chapter 2: Introduction to Graphing Equations

    Last chapter we learned about equations and how to solve for x. This chapter we will learn how to graph an equation. Graphing an equation means to plot all the points on a coordinate plane and connect them to form lines, curves, and many other shapes.

    Let's talk about an example: y = 2x + 3. This equation is in slope-intercept form, which means that the number 2 is the slope and the number 3 is the y-intercept. To plot this into a graph, we first start at the y-intercept (3). Then to do the slope, you need to understand rise over run — how much the line goes up or down over how much it goes left or right. Since the slope is 2, that means the line goes up 2 for every 1 it goes to the right. Think about it this way:

    2 = 2/1 = 2 rise / 1 run

    You can continue plotting a few points following that pattern, draw a straight line through them, and you’ll have your graph of the equation!
