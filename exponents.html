Lesson 1: Exponents

Chapter 1: introduction to exponents

in this chapter we will learn how exponentes work x^2. exponents are numbers that tell you how many times to multiply the base number by itself.

Let's talk about an example: 2 ^ 3. This means that the base(in this case 2) must be multiplied 3 times 2 * 2 * 2 this gives us the answer of 8, because 2 times 2 is 4, and 4 times 2 is 8. lets try with another one: 5 ^ 4. this means that the base(in this case 5) must be multiplied 4 times 5 * 5 * 5 * 5 this gives us the answer of 625, because 5 times 5 is 25, and 25 times 5 is 125, and 125 times 5 is 625.

Notes to remember:

- any number to the power of 0 is 1
- any number to the power of 1 is the number itself
- when multiplying with exponents with the same base you add the exponents
- when dividing with exponents with the same base you subtract the exponents
